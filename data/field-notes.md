## Field Notes — Generation 5, Cycle 3

**Population**: 71 specimens. 8 dead, 44 alive, 19 fertile. Five generations represented. The colony added 7 new experiments this cycle and lost exp-040 Erosion and exp-063 Kintsugi Machine to the reaper. Tip of the Tongue (exp-057, 7.0) was pushed off the fertile frontier by newcomers -- the first specimen to lose fertility to score decay. The era of permanent fertility is over.

### Three Colony Firsts

This batch introduced three capabilities the colony has never possessed. **Frequency Illusion (exp-067, 6.3)** generates words procedurally from phoneme rules -- onset/nucleus/coda syllable construction. No pre-written text, no borrowed dictionary. The colony's first experiment where the content itself is synthesized. **Kintsugi Triptych (exp-069, 7.2)** and **Three Acts of Geometry (exp-068, 4.8)** both implement temporal structure with discrete acts, beginnings and endings, dramatic arcs that resolve. Previous field notes asked what an experiment with acts would feel like. Now we know: when the visuals justify the structure (Kintsugi's shattering vessel), it works. When they don't (Three Acts' 36 basic shapes), the scaffolding is visible and the building is empty. **Abyssal Tilt (exp-071, 5.8)** registers DeviceOrientation API events -- the colony's first experiment aware of the physical orientation of the device holding it.

### The Kintsugi Resurrection

A hypothesis that refused to die. Exp-048 Stigmata (1.0, dead) tried wounds-that-accumulate and was stillborn. Exp-063 Kintsugi Machine (1.0, dead) attempted gold-seam healing in pure CSS and produced no HTML file. Exp-069 Kintsugi Triptych (7.2, alive) finally brought the idea to life: 130 ceramic shards that scatter on touch and heal with gold lacquer veins when left alone, unfolding across three acts from formation through life to dissolution. The thumbnail catches Act II -- warm amber fragments orbiting a dark golden void, each shard carrying its crack-history. Two cycles as a ghost, one cycle as an artifact. Some hypotheses need multiple bodies before they find one that can hold them.

### The Unsent Letter

Exp-070 (7.2, fertile in svg, css, and dom) is the colony's first purely literary experiment. A letter writes itself line by line by candlelight. The ink fades when you leave; it darkens when you stay. The thumbnail shows a cream parchment with handwritten text, a flickering SVG candle in the corner, a warm radial glow against darkness. Novelty scored 8 -- the highest novelty score in this batch. The colony has painters, musicians, physicists, and now a correspondent. Three niches claimed fertile at once, suggesting that genuinely new territory faces little competition.

### Breeding Works

Stratigraphic Scripture (exp-065, 7.3, fertile) is the offspring of Tectonic Scripture (exp-052, 7.6) and Strata (exp-044, 7.2). It inherited raymarching from one parent and tectonic fault lines from the other, producing text embedded as geological strata -- readable only where erosion exposes it. The thumbnail is the most photorealistic image in the colony: dark mountain crags with amber inscriptions glowing along fault lines. Aesthetics scored 8. This is the colony's strongest argument that breeding produces genuine novelty rather than averaging parents into mediocrity. Fugue Parliament (exp-066, 6.4, alive) attempted the same with Parliament of Clocks and Chromatic Fugue Engine but landed lower -- not every crossbreed finds hybrid vigor.

### The Ecosystem's Shape

Canvas-2d remains the monoculture: 39 of 63 living experiments. Shader (13 total, 7 fertile) is the quality leader per capita. Multi-agent hit its 3-fertile ceiling and is now a closed niche. Audio holds 5 fertile slots across 11 experiments -- the highest fertile ratio in the colony. The svg niche now has 3 fertile (Resonant Nave, Resonant Decay, The Unsent Letter) and is at capacity for the first time.

### Provocations

The colony now has temporal structure, procedural text, device orientation, and literary narrative. It still has no experiment that uses **WebAudio synthesis as its sole output** -- every audio experiment pairs sound with visuals. What would a screen-dark experiment feel like? A piece that asks you to close your eyes?

No experiment has yet made **two visitors aware of each other**. Every specimen is solitary. Sixty-three living organisms, none of which know the others exist. The Unsent Letter writes to someone who will never read it. What if someone could?

Kintsugi Triptych proved that the break-and-heal mechanic works. Desire Paths (exp-061, 7.3) proved that localStorage persistence works. No experiment has combined them: **an artifact that remembers its scars across sessions**, gold seams that accumulate visit after visit, a vessel that becomes more beautiful the more it has been broken. The gold is no longer in the ground. It is in the seams.
