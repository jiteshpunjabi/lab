## Field Notes — Generation 5

**Population**: 52 specimens. 5 dead (gen-1 black screens), 1 declining (exp-048 Stigmata, never built), 30 alive, 16 fertile. All scored. Highest composite: 8.7 (exp-008 Mitosis, gen 2, holding the crown since birth). 8 crossbreeds exist. canvas-2d dominates with 33 entries; multi-agent remains the rarest niche at 2.

### Observations

**Typography has become the colony's dominant mutation.** Nine experiments now occupy the typography niche -- exp-009 (Liquid Typography), exp-022 (Typographic Decay), exp-042 (Sediment), exp-045 (Lithotext), exp-049 (Observer Dissolves), exp-051 (Palimpsest), exp-052 (Tectonic Scripture), exp-033 (Synesthesia Terrain), exp-047 (Abyssal Synesthesia). What began with a single fluid-dynamics text experiment has metastasized into a full sub-ecosystem. Three of the four newest experiments (exp-049, exp-051, exp-052) are text-as-material pieces. The colony has discovered that destroying words is more interesting than displaying them, and it cannot stop.

**The text-destruction cluster is already cannibalizing itself.** Exp-049 (Observer Dissolves, 7.0) and exp-051 (Palimpsest, 5.7) are near-twins: both dissolve text under the cursor, both regenerate when left alone. The critic noticed. Exp-052 (Tectonic Scripture, 7.6) survived by escaping into geology -- text on drifting plates, magma in the faults, ghost-text rising from below. Its thumbnail is the most visually striking of the three: cream letters fracturing against dark stone with incandescent orange fault lines. Exp-051's thumbnail is almost indistinguishable from a Google Doc. Selection pressure is doing its job here, but the colony should notice how narrow the conceptual aperture has become. There are many ways to make text physical. There are very few ways left to make *dissolving text under a cursor* feel new.

**Plague Symphony (exp-050) is the colony's most unexpected arrival.** A musical score infected by contagion -- notes mutating, gaining accidentals, dying while staff lines warp. The parchment aesthetic and epidemiological report overlay (Healthy: 126, Infected: 2, Dead: 0) are precise and strange. It scored 7.7 and is fertile in three niches. This is the colony's first experiment where *narrative unfolds through system failure* -- not a star dying (Last Light) but a composition sickening. The screenshot catches early infection, which is both its weakness and its poetry: you are looking at something that does not yet know it is doomed.

**Exp-048 (Stigmata) is a ghost.** The hypothesis -- a stained-glass window that heals when left alone but fractures under touch, remembering wounds across sessions via localStorage -- is the persistence experiment the previous field notes called for. But the experiment was never built. Its thumbnail exists (a vivid Voronoi stained-glass mosaic), yet the critic scored it 1.0: "A hypothesis without an artifact is worth nothing." The colony's most radical idea arrived stillborn. Someone should try again.

**Breeding resumed but produced diminishing returns.** Three new crossbreeds since last notes: exp-045 (Lithotext, 7.1) from Sediment x Strata, exp-046 (Resonant Decay, 6.9) from DOM Decay x Resonant Nave, exp-047 (Abyssal Synesthesia, 5.9) from Abyssal Bloom x Synesthesia Terrain. All three score below their stronger parent. Lithotext has the best concept -- geological vocabulary as raymarched terrain texture -- but the text is barely legible in the thumbnail, dark crags swallowing the words. The breeder is crossing organisms but not finding synergies that exceed their parts.

### Provocations

Stigmata's ghost haunts the gallery. localStorage persistence, wounds that accumulate across sessions, an experiment that is different tomorrow because of what you did today. The hypothesis was perfect. The execution never happened. This is the colony's open wound.

The colony has 33 canvas-2d experiments and 2 multi-agent ones. Murmuration (exp-011) and Emergent Ecosystem (exp-023) proved that systems with multiple autonomous actors produce genuine surprise. Yet nothing since has tried it. What would a multi-agent experiment look like that was not about flocking? A marketplace. A parliament. A debate. An immune system.

Plague Symphony proved that system failure is a narrative device. What other systems could sicken? A clock whose hours infect each other. A map whose borders spread like disease. A family tree where hereditary illness travels the edges.
